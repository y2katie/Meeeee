Amplicity

QA Training Platform

2021 - 2022
Brand Web Design System

Project Overview

A website created to train QA engineers, intentionally coded with accessibility errors. The experience uses gamification, encouraging learners to spot issues and check them off as they progress.

Role

Full-Stack Developer & UX Designer

Duration

8 months

Team

QA Training Team

Tools

React, Node.js, WCAG Guidelines

The Challenge

QA engineers needed hands-on experience identifying accessibility issues, but traditional training materials were theoretical and didn't provide realistic scenarios. We needed a way to simulate real-world accessibility problems in a controlled learning environment.

The Solution

We built a fully functional website intentionally embedded with common accessibility violations. QA engineers could explore the site, use assistive technologies, and identify issues in a gamified environment that tracked their progress and provided immediate feedback.

Key Features

  • Intentionally flawed website with 50+ accessibility violations
  • Progressive difficulty levels from basic to advanced issues
  • Real-time scoring and achievement system
  • Integrated screen reader and keyboard navigation testing
  • Detailed explanations for each accessibility violation
  • Team leaderboards and collaborative challenges

Technical Implementation

The platform was built with React and Node.js, featuring a sophisticated tracking system that monitored user interactions and provided analytics on learning patterns. We carefully crafted accessibility violations that represented real-world scenarios while ensuring the learning experience remained accessible.

Impact

The platform trained over 200 QA engineers across multiple organizations. Post-training assessments showed a 95% improvement in accessibility issue detection rates, and the gamified approach achieved a 92% completion rate compared to 35% for traditional training methods.
