Journey Mapping

Accessibility Experience Research

2021
Product Brand Web

Project Overview

A comprehensive user journey mapping project focused on understanding and improving the accessibility experience across digital touchpoints. This research-driven initiative mapped the complete user experience for individuals with disabilities navigating digital services.

Role

UX Researcher & Service Designer

Duration

5 months

Research Participants

45 users with diverse abilities

Tools

Miro, UserVoice, Journey Mapping

The Challenge

Organizations often design digital experiences without fully understanding the complete journey of users with disabilities. This leads to fragmented experiences, accessibility barriers, and missed opportunities to create truly inclusive services.

Research Approach

We conducted in-depth interviews and observational studies with 45 participants representing various disabilities and assistive technology usage patterns. The research spanned multiple touchpoints including websites, mobile apps, customer service, and physical locations.

Key Methodology

  • Contextual interviews in participants' natural environments
  • Task-based observations with assistive technologies
  • Emotional journey mapping to capture frustration and delight moments
  • Cross-channel experience analysis
  • Collaborative mapping sessions with stakeholders
  • Accessibility audit integration with user feedback

Key Findings

The research revealed critical gaps in the user experience, including inconsistent accessibility implementations across channels, lack of alternative pathways when digital solutions failed, and significant emotional impact of accessibility barriers on user confidence and trust.

Deliverables

  • Comprehensive accessibility journey maps for 5 key user personas
  • Pain point prioritization matrix with business impact analysis
  • Opportunity identification for accessibility improvements
  • Service blueprint highlighting backend accessibility requirements
  • Implementation roadmap with quick wins and long-term initiatives

Impact

The journey mapping research led to a comprehensive accessibility improvement program, resulting in a 75% reduction in user-reported accessibility issues and a 40% increase in task completion rates for users with disabilities. The methodology has since been adopted as a standard practice for inclusive design research.
