Empathy Mapping

UX UI Accessible Best Practices

2023 - 2024
Product Design System Brand Web

Project Overview

An interactive exercise where participants took on personas and worked through basic login screens to reinforce accessibility best practices from a UX/UI perspective.

Role

UX Researcher & Workshop Facilitator

Duration

6 weeks

Participants

25 UX/UI Designers

Tools

Miro, Figma, User Personas

The Challenge

Many UX/UI designers understand accessibility in theory but struggle to apply it practically in their daily work. We needed a hands-on approach that would help designers truly understand the user experience from different perspectives, particularly for users with disabilities.

The Solution

We created an empathy mapping exercise where designers adopted specific personas with different accessibility needs and navigated through common login interfaces. This experiential learning approach helped designers understand the real impact of their design decisions.

Workshop Structure

  • Persona assignment with detailed accessibility profiles
  • Guided navigation through various login interfaces
  • Real-time empathy mapping of frustrations and successes
  • Group discussion and insight sharing
  • Actionable takeaways for immediate implementation

Key Insights

Participants reported a significant shift in perspective, with 90% saying they now consider accessibility from the beginning of their design process rather than as an afterthought. The workshop led to the adoption of new accessibility guidelines across multiple design teams.

Impact

The empathy mapping workshop became a standard part of onboarding for new designers and has been replicated across 15+ teams. Post-workshop audits showed a 60% improvement in accessibility compliance across new design deliverables.
